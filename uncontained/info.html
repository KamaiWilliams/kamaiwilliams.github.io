ECOSTYSTEMS
& CYCLES OF LIFE

This project explores the cycle of accessibility and neglect within New York City’s
public infrastructure by focusing on something as essential and human as the need for restrooms.
Through data visualization, it highlights how public spaces are designed, maintained, and forgotten.

Data Ethics & Considerations

  • Provenance: All datasets originate from official NYC Open Data sources maintained by city agencies.
  • Representativeness: Data reflects city-managed facilities and monitored intersections only. Private or unrecorded spaces are not included.
  • Known Bias / Limits: Restroom dataset may exclude temporarily closed or unlisted facilities. Pedestrian data is from limited sensors.
  • Sensitive Fields: No personal identifying information (PII) was used. All data are aggregated and public.

Urban data often reveals what’s missing as much as what’s measured. By mapping restrooms
and foot traffic, this project visualizes how public needs are both seen and unseen,
asking how infrastructure can evolve to meet the rhythms of real city life.
